3. Examples of traditional culture

3.1. Holidays - Seollal

  • first day of Korean calendar
  • traditional and national holiday
  • celebrated by Koreans all around the world
  • visiting family (고향 방문)
  • performing ancestral rites (jesa, 제사)
  • eating tteokguk, jeon, japchae, and yakgwa
  • playing folk games
  • bowing to elders (saebae, 새배)
  • banned during Japanese occupation (1907-1945)
  • re-designated as a national holiday in late 1980s

3.2. Arts - mask dance (talchum, 탈춤)

  • performed wearing masks
  • combines dancing and singing
  • name originates from Hwanghae Province
  • often features drama and story
  • portrays people, animals, and supernatural creatures
  • reflects lower classes' frustrations towards the elites
  • common themes:
    • exorcisms
    • rituals
    • satire
    • parody
    • evils
    • elites
    • Buddhist monks
    • shamans
    • wifes and concubines
  • performed on First Full Moon (jeongwol daeboreum, 정월 대보름), Buddha's Birthday, Danoje, and Chuseok
