1. Types of sports in Korea

1.1. Traditional sports

1.1.1. Martial arts

  • dangsudo (당수도)
    • founded in 1944
    • based on karate mixed with taekkyeon, subakdo, and northern Chinese martial arts
    • uses both bare hands and legs
    • provided the foundation for taekwondo
  • geomdo (검도)
    • founded in the late 19th century
    • Korean version of kendo
    • faught with bamboo swords
    • uses armour for body protection
  • hapgido (합기도)
    • founded in 1946
    • mixed-marial art derived from Japanese Daitō-ryū Jujutsu
    • often confused with modern Japanese aikido
    • harmony between soft and hard techniques
  • hwarangdo (화랑도)
    • developed in the 1960s
    • based on ancient Silla's warriors called Hwarang
    • faught both bare-handed and with weapons
    • focused on intellectual and character development
  • taekwondo (태권도)
    • founded in 1944
    • combination of fast arm and leg movement
    • the most popular modern Korean martial art
    • a full medal Olympic sport since 2000
  • taekkyeon (태껸/택견)
    • derived from folk games during the Joseon period
    • first named in writing in the late 18th century
    • rediscovered after the Japanese occupation and Korean War
    • enlisted in UNESCO World Heritage as Intangible Cultural Property of South Korea
    • focused on rhythmic and fluid leg movement with arms support

1.1.2. Artistic sports

  • juldarigi (줄다리기)
    • Korean-style tug of war
    • derived from one of the folk games
    • registered as international olympic dyscipline
    • enlisted in UNESCO World Heritage
  • jultagi (줄타기)
  • masangjae (마상재)
    • presenting various talents while riding a horse
    • existed since at least the early Goguryeo period
    • learnt by all horse knights by the late 18th century
  • yeonnalligi (연날리기)

1.1.3. Ball games

  • gyeokgu (격구)
    • introduced into China, Korea, and Japan from ancient Persia
    • played on a horseback similarly to modern polo
    • enjoyed mainly by the aristocracy and royal court

1.1.4. Other

  • gungdo (궁도)
    • traditional archery
    • a wooden bow paired with a bamboo string
    • traditionally used as a weapon since at least 20,000 years ago
    • emphasises mental development and etiquette
  • ssireum (씨름)
    • folk wrestling since the 4th century
    • bringing the opponent down without inflicting harm
    • popular both among the commoners and the military
    • revival and acknowledgement as a sport in the 20th century
    • World Ssireum Championships held since 2008
    • sometimes confused with Japanese sumo

1.2. Modern sports

1.2.1. Martial arts

  • boxing (격투기)

1.2.2. Ball games

  • baseball (야구)
  • football (축구)
  • basketball (배구)
  • volleyball (배구)
  • jokgu (족구)
    • Korean-style combination of football and volleyball
    • invented by the South Korean military in 1960
    • promotion of exercise and sport among soldiers
    • very popular in physical education classes at school

1.2.3. Racket games

  • tenis
  • table tennis (탁구)
  • badminton

1.2.4. Winter sports

  • skiing
  • ice skating
  • figure skating
  • ice hockey
  • curling

1.2.5. Mind sports

  • baduk (바둑)
    • also known as Go in Japan and Weiqi in China
  • chess
  • janggi (장기)
    • also called “Korean chess”

1.2.6. Other

  • Western archery (양궁)
  • golf
  • billiard (당구)
  • horse riding (승마)

1.2. E-sports

  1. First stage (1998-2010)
    • began with tournaments in RTS, sports, and fighting games in the mid-1990s:
      • Command & Conquer Red Alert
      • WarCraft II
      • Dark Reign:The Future of War
      • FIFA
      • Tekken (철권)
    • exploded in popularity with the release of StarCraft: Brood War in 1998
    • concentrated around PC bangs (Internet cafes)
    • popular not only among teenagers but all age groups
    • investments and sponsorships from large Korean companies
    • development from amateur gaming into professional gaming
    • treating computer games on a similar level to regular sports
  2. Second stage (2010-2020)
    • decline in popularity due to multiple match fixing scandals
    • switch from StarCraft to League of Legends
    • less interest from the general public
  3. Third stage (2020-present)
    • financial hit due to the COVID-19 pandemic
