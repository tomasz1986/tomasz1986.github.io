1. Basic information

  • Official name: Democratic People's Republic of Korea (조선민주주의인민공화국)
  • Capital: Pyongyang (평양)
  • Government: one-party state led by a dynastic totalitarian dictatorship
  • Population: 25 million
  • Area: 120,538 km2
  • Life expectancy:
    • men - 66,6 years
    • women - 74,5 years
  • Ethnicity:
    • 99.8% - Korean
    • 0.2% - Chinese
  • Workforce:
    • 63% - industry & services
    • 37% - agriculture
  • Export/Import partner: China - 76%
  • GDP per capita: 1,200 USD
  • Official language: Korean (munhwao, 문화어)
  • Official script: Chosongul (조선글)
  • Religion:
    • 73% - no religion
    • 13% - Chondoism (천도교)
    • 12% - Shamanism
    • 1.5% - Buddhism

4. Kim dynasty

  1. Kim Il Sung
    • born April 15, 1912 near Pyongyang
    • leader of North Korea from 1948 to 1994
      • premier (1948 - 1972)
      • head of state (1972-1994)
      • chairman of Korean Workers' Party (1949-1994)
    • attended elementary school in Manchuria
    • arrested and jailed for youth communist organisation activities in 1929-1930
    • joined anti-Japanese communist guerilla resistance in the 1930s
    • received military and political training in the Soviet Union
    • selected by Joseph Stalin as the leader of the new North Korean state
    • ignited the Korean War in 1950 aiming to reunify the country
    • crushed internal opposition and became absolute ruler in the 1950s
    • developed juche (주체) ideology of self-reliance
    • strict policy of state-ruled planned economy with no private ownership
    • used propaganda to establish his own legend and promote state ideology
    • cultivated close ties with USSR and PRC, and hostile relations with ROK and USA
    • died in 1992 just two weeks before planned North-South Korean summit in Pyongyang
    • posthumously titled eternal president (영원한 수령)
  2. Kim Jong Il
    • born February 16, 1941 in Siberia, USSR
    • leader of North Korea from 1994 to 2011
    • relocated to China for safety during the Korean War
    • attended pilots training college in East Germany
    • graduated from Kim Il Sung University in 1963
    • served numerous posts in Korean Workers' Party
    • became his father's secretary and worked closely together in the 1960s
    • appointed as party secretary of organisation, propaganda, and agitation in 1973
    • officially designated as Kim Il Sung's successor in 1980
    • commander of armed forced and important figure in the party from 1990
    • de facto leader of North Korea from 1994, officially from 1997/1998
    • ruled as chairman of the National Defense Commission (국방위원장)
    • prioritised the army with military-first policy (songun, 선군정치)
    • tried to improve foreign relations and North-South relations
    • developed and conducted 1st underground nuclear test in 2006
    • died suddenly on a train on December 19, 2011
  3. Kim Jong Un
    • born January 8, 1984 (?) in North Korea
    • current leader of North Korea since 2011
    • the youngest of Kim Jong Il's three sons
    • educated at the International School of Berne in Switzerland
    • attended Kim Il Sung National War College in 2002-2007
    • worked with his father in Korean Workers' Party and the army
    • unofficially designated as his father's successor in 2009
    • began assuming important party and state positions since 2009
    • declared North Korea's leader after Kim Jong Il's death in 2011
    • consolidated power and officially solidified his position in 2011-2016
    • ruthlessly purged powerful party members to remove potential opposition
    • accelerated ballistic missile tests and nuclear programme development
    • tried to improve North-South relations using 2018 Winter Olympic Games in Pyeongchang
    • for the first time in history met face-to-face with United States president in 2018
    • since 2022 has been strengthening the strategic partnership with Russia

5. Poland-North Korea relations

  • diplomatic ties established on October 16, 1948
  • academic cooperation since the 1950s (at Warsaw University)
  • one of the few countries maintaining diplomatic and trading relations with DPRK
  • maintain embassies in Warsaw and Pyongyang
  • official government and party delegation visits in the 1950s
  • 1,400 North Korean orphans housed in Poland during the Korean War
  • Poland as a member of Neutral Nations Supervisory Commission from 1953 to 1993
  • maintained symbolic-level trade relations between 1967 and 2018
  • lost of friend status and privileged position by Poland after 1989
  • official high level visits:
    • 1956 - Premier Kim Il Sung in Poland
    • 1984 - President Kim Il Sung in Poland
    • 1986 - Chairman Wojciech Jaruzelski in Korea
    • 2001, 2004, 2007 - Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs of Poland in Korea
    • 2008 - Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs of the DPRK in Poland
