Legacy and Influence of Confucian Culture on Modern Korean Society

Korean Language

1. Honorifics

Types of honorifics (높임법 종류)

  • personal (상대 높임법)
    • split into 반말 and 존댓말
    • used by the speaker to the listener
    • the most important honorifics in Korean
    • e.g. 가세요/가라
  • subject (주체 높임법)
    • forms used about the subject of a conversation
    • the second most important honorifics in Korean
    • e.g. 그 사람/그분
  • object (객체 높임법)
    • adjectives and verbs used in relation to the object
    • less important than personal and subject honorifics
    • e.g. 드리다, 여쭙다
  • lexical (어휘 높임법)
    • vocabulary used depending on the person, e.g. 밥/진지, 집/댁
  • morphological (형태 높임법)
    • grammatical endings added to parts of speech, e.g. 가다/가시다, 부모/부모님께서

Levels of personal honorifics (상대 높임법)

  • formal (격식체)
    • 존댓말
      • very polite (아주높임): 하십시오체, e.g. 갑니다, 갑니까, 가십시오
      • moderately polite (예사높임): 하오체, e.g. 갑시다, 가시오
    • 반말
      • moderately impolite (예사낮춤): 하게체, e.g. 가게
      • very impolite (아주낮춤): 해라체, e.g. 가라
  • informal (비격식체)
    • 존댓말
      • universally polite (두루높임): 해요체, e.g. 가요
    • 반말
      • universally impolite (두루낮춤): 해체, e.g. 가

Relative honorifics (압존법)

  • depend on the relation between the subject and the listener (instead of the speaker)
  • e.g. 할머니, 어머니가 어디에 있어요?

2. Titles

“Title” in English:
a name such as ‘Sir’ or ‘Professor’, or abbreviations such as ‘Mrs’ or ‘Dr’, that are used before someone’s name to show their rank or profession, whether they are married etc (Longman Dictionary of Contemporary English)
“호칭” in Korean:
화자가 대화의 상대를 부를 때 사용하는 표현. (한국민족문화백과사전)

Characteristics of titles in Korean

  • describe the relations between the speaker and the listener
  • mirror and adapt to the current social structure
  • improper usage does not make communication impossible (in contrary to grammar and vocabulary)
  • proper or improper usage may have lasting effect on personal relations
  • extensively developed and complex (in comparison with other languages)
  • inseparably connected with specific grammatical and lexical forms, e.g.
    1. 민수야, 밥 먹어라.
    2. 누나, 밥 드세요.
    3. 교수님, 점심 잡수세요.
    4. 할아버지, 진지 자십시오.
  • depend on many factors, e.g. level of kinship, social position, age, gender, knowledge about the person in question, place of the discussion, etc

Main types of titles in Korean

  • family relations
  • social relations
  • workplace relations

Family relations

  • age, level of kinship, and gender as the main factors
  • maternal vs paternal relatives, e.g.
    • maternal: 외할아버지, 외할머니
    • paternal: 친할아버지, 친할머니
  • close vs distant family, e.g.
    • 동생 (younger brother or sister)
    • 삼촌 동생 (cousin)
    • 사촌 동생 (cousin)
    • 오촌 동생 (cousin)
    • 육촌 동생 (cousin)
  • biological kinship (blood relations) vs social relations, e.g.
    • 어머니 (biological mother)
    • 시어머니 (mother-in-law, husband's mother)
    • 큰어머니 (wife of father's older brother)
    • 작은어머니 (wife of father's younger brother)
    • 의붓어머니 (step-mother)
    • 양어머니 (adoptive mother)

Social relations

  • age, social position, level of intimacy, and gender as the main factors

Personal titles (이름 호칭)

  • older person to younger person
  • between people of the same age
  • exceptions:
    • so-called 빠른 생일 (person born between in January or February)
    • relationship where the woman is older than the man
  • 성-이름+님: 김지은님
  • (성)-이름+씨: 김지은 씨, 지은 씨
  • 성+씨: 김 씨
  • 성-이름+군/양: 김종수 군, 김지은 양
  • 이름(이): 종수, 지은이
  • 이름아/야: 종수야, 지은아

Direct titles (직접 호칭)

  • 당신
    • 당신, 요즘 직장에서 피곤하시죠? (between spouses)
    • 당신이 꼭 알아야 할 사실들. (written instructions)
    • 당신이 뭔데 참견이야? (aggressively to a stranger)
  • 자네 (older person to young person; more polite than 너)
    • 자네가 나를 불렀는가?
    • 자네는 이름이 뭐지?
  • 너 (older person to younger person; between people of the same age)
    • 너, 이리 좀 와봐라.
    • 누가 너를 데리고 왔니?

Social titles

  • polite forms used to other people, e.g.
    • 사장님/사모님 instead of 아저씨/아줌마
    • 선생님 instead of 아저씨/아줌마
  • 성(이름)+직함+님: 김(지은) 사장님
  • 성(이름)+직함: 김(지은) 사장
  • 성(이름)+선생(님): 김(지은) 선생(님)

Family-related titles

  • forms used in everyday life to people not related by blood, e.g.
    • 할아버지/할머니 (old man / woman)
    • 아저씨/아재 (middle-aged or married man)
    • 아주머니/아줌마 (middle-aged or married woman)
    • 아가씨/총각 (young woman / young man)
    • 삼촌/이모 (uncle/aunt)
    • 언니/오빠 (not much older woman or an older female friend / not much older man or older male friend)
    • 누나/형 (not much older woman or an older female friend / not much older man or older male friend)

Workplace relations

  • workplace position as the main factor
  • calling by title only, surname + title, surname + name + title, but not name + title, e.g.
    • 교수님
    • 김 교수님
    • 김종수 교수님
    • 종수 교수님
