Legacy and Influence of Confucian Culture on Modern Korean Society

History of Confucianism in Korea

Traditional Confucianism

  • myth of Gija Joseon (11th century BC)
  • myth of Gojoseon (5th century BC)
  • influx of Yan people from Liaodong region during Han dynasty (since 202 BC)
  • establishment of Wissi Joseon in 194 BC by Wiman from Yan people
  • invasion of Wissi Joseon by Emperor Wu of Han China in 108 BC
    • establishment of four commanderies
  • Three Kingdoms
    • Goguryo, Baekje, Silla, Gaya
    • Goguryeo expanding to Liaohe River on Liaoning peninsula
    • eradication of Chinese commanderies in 313 AD by Goguryeo and Baekje
    • transmission of Confucianism into Goguryeo and Baekje
    • establishment of National Confucian Academy by King Sosurim of Goguryeo in 372 AD
    • transmission of Buddhism from China to Goguryeo in 372 AD
    • widespread use of Chinese characters in Goguryeo
      • biography of King Gwanggaeto the Great on his tomb
    • wars between Goguryeo and Sui/Tang China (6-7th century)
    • introduction of Confucianism to Japan from Baekje in 513
    • establishment of relations between Silla and China in 532
    • peaceful coexistence of Confucianism and Buddhism in Silla
      • promotion of Confucian virtues by monk Wongwang
      • integration of Confucian virtues into Hwarangdo
    • subjugation of Baekje in 660 and Goguryeo in 668 by Silla
