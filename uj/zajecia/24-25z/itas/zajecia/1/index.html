1. What is Asia?

  • geographical Asia created and imposed by Ancient Greeks
  • ancient Greek word Ἀσία (first used by Herodotus in 440 BCE)
  • reference to the Bronze Age region of Assuwa (modern Anatolia in Turkey)
  • province of Asia in the Roman Empire (western Anatolia)
  • historical and cultural construct (East vs West)
  • Western countries always attracted to China and India
  • Silk Road as the main East-West trading route
  • birthplace of the majority of world religions

1.1. World continent models

  • 7-continent model (Africa, Asia, Europe, North America, South America, Antarctica, Australia)
  • 6-continent model (Africa, Asia, Europe, America, Antarctica, Australia)
  • 6-continent model (Africa, Eurasia, North America, South America, Antarctica, Australia)
  • 5-continent model (Africa, Eurasia, America, Antarctica, Australia)
  • 4-continent model (Afro-Eurasia, America, Antarctica, Australia)

1.2. Boundaries of Asia

  • Asia-Africa boundary: Red Sea, Gulf of Suez, Suez Canal
  • Asia-Europe boundary: Caucasus Mountains
  • Asia-Australia boundary: Maluku Islands (Indonesia)
  • Asia-America boundary: Bering Strait, Bering Sea

1.3. Regions of Asia

  • North Asia
  • Central Asia
  • West Asia (Middle East, Caucasus)
  • South Asia (India)
  • East Asia (Far East)
  • Southeast Asia

2. Statistics of Asia

2.1. The largest contintent in the world

  • area of 45 mln km2 (9% of total surface area, 30% of total land)
  • the longest coastline of 62,800 km
  • the tallest mountain range in the world (Himalayas)
  • extremely diverse climate (arctic, subarctic, tropical, moist, dry)
  • very heavily impacted by the climate change

2.2. The most populous continent in the world

  • population of 4.7 billion (60% of total world population)
  • China and India alone estimated at over 2.8 billion
  • projected to grow to 5.25 billion by 2055 (54% of world population)
  • many high population density areas (Singapore, Bangladesh, Taiwan, South Korea, India)

2.3. The second largest number of states in the world

  • 49 states recognised by the United Nations
  • 2 partially recognised states (Palestine, Taiwan)
  • 3 unrecognised states (Abkhazia, Northern Cyprus, South Ossetia)
  • 4 overseas territories (British, Australian)
  • 2 special areas (Hong Kong, Macau)

2.4. The largest contintental economy in the world

  • 37% of global economic output
  • China comprising half of Asia's GDP
  • 7 out of 20 world's largest economies (China, Japan, India, South Korea, Indonesia, Saudi Arabia, Turkey)
  • rapid economic growth since the mid-late 20th century
  • very rich in natural resources

2.5. multiple language families and isolating languages

  • Sino-Tibetan (Chinese, Tibetan, Burmese)
  • Indo-European (Indo-Aryan, Russian, Greek, Armenian)
  • Altaic (Turkish, Mongolian, Korean, Japanese)
  • Mon–Khmer (Vietnamese, Khmer)
  • Kra–Dai (Thai, Lao)
  • Austronesian (Tagalog, Malay)
  • Dravidian (Telugu, Tamil)
  • Afro-Asiatic (Arabic, Hebrew)
  • Siberian (Uralic languages, Ainu)
  • Caucasian (Georgian, Chechen)

3. Korea's place in Asia

  • peninsular region located in East Asia (Far East)
  • extends 1,100 km from mainland into the Pacific Ocean
  • total size of 223,155 km2 (including islands)
  • surrounded by Yellow Sea and Sea of Japan (East Sea)
  • separated from China by Yalu/Amnok and Tumen/Duman Rivers, and Russia by Tumen/Duman River
  • two sovereign Korean states existing since 1948
  • the kingdom of Goryeo as the origin of the name Korea
  • varied topography and geographical features
    • plains, mountains, rivers
    • 70% covered by mountains
    • plains in the south-west, mountains in the north-east
    • Mount Paektu as the tallest mountain, sacred to all Koreans
    • Nakdong River as the longest river in South Korea
  • warm and wet climate in the South, colder climate in the South
  • human inhabition since 10,000 BCE, settlement since 6,000 BCE
  • first Korean state of Gojoseon established in 2333 BCE
  • populated by 76 million people (51 million in the South, 25 million in the North)
  • Seoul Metropolitan Area alone populated by 25 million people
  • totalitarian dictatorship in the North, liberal democracy in the South
  • state-controlled economy in the North, capitalist economy in the South
  • very large economic discrepancy between North and South
